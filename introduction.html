Introduction to Dashain

The Festival of Dashain

What Is Dashain?

Dashain or Bada'dashain, also referred as Vijaya Dashami in Sanskrit, is a major Hindu religious festival in Nepal and the Indian states of Sikkim, West Bengal, Assam, South India, and Sri Lanka. It is also celebrated by other religions of Nepal and elsewhere in the world, including among the Lhotshampa of Bhutan and the Burmese Gurkhas of Myanmar. The festival is also referred as Nauratha, derived from the Sanskrit word for the same festival Navaratri which translates to Nine Nights. It is the longest and the most auspicious festival in the Bikram Sambat and Nepal Sambat annual calendars, celebrated by Nepali Hindus, along with their diaspora throughout the globe. In Nepal, it is also known as the biggest festival in the country and is the longest national/public holiday, lasting 15 days. It is the most anticipated festival in Nepal.

How is Dashain Celebrated?

Goddess Durga, symbolizing valor and prowess, is worshipped and offered sacrifices to ensure the devotees' progress and prosperity. During the first ten days, pilgrims flock to various river confluences early in the morning and sacred shrines in the evening. Ghatasthapana, Phool Pati, Mahaastami, Nawami and Vijaya Dashami are the series of the events under Dashain each marked with a different set of rituals.

The Significance of Dashain

Why is the Dashain Important in Hindu culture?

Dashain is celebrated after Goddess Durga defeats Mahishasura, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. One of the major festivals of Nepal is also the Longest ritual that people do. They worship Goddess Durga during Dashain for prosperity and happiness. It brings families together, and people celebrate it with tika and receive blessings from elders while sharing meals and laughter, making wonderful memories. People clean their homes and decorate them to create a welcoming atmosphere for Goddess Durga, symbolizing the removal of negativity and the renewal of life. Indirectly, Dashain also boosts the country’s economy as the demand for goods increases. Places use vehicles and transportation and even purchase gifts for their relatives, increasing the country’s overall sales.
